What Is Penetrating Damp?

Penetrating damp is water entering your property horizontally through external walls, roofs, window frames or other building elements. It can appear at any height and worsens during or after heavy rain.

Note: Do not just treat the symptoms. Painting over damp patches does not fix penetrating damp — it hides it temporarily while the damage continues.

Common Causes

  • Failed or eroded pointing between bricks or stonework
  • Cracked or porous render on external walls
  • Faulty or blocked guttering causing water to run down walls
  • Leaking or damaged flat roofs above rooms
  • Failed window or door seals and frames
  • Cracked chimney stacks or failed flashings
